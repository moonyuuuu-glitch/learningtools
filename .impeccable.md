## Design Context

### Users

This is a personal, desktop-first knowledge workspace used over a long period of time. Its primary job is to preserve learning materials, help useful frameworks resurface without creating review pressure, and make relationships between ideas understandable and retrievable.

### Brand Personality

Quiet, deliberate, and scholarly. The interface should feel like a private research desk: trustworthy enough for long-term notes, calm enough for daily use, and precise enough to explain why knowledge is connected.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a restrained light theme with warm, subtly tinted neutrals and a small number of semantic accents. Favor editorial hierarchy, stable spatial memory, and paper-like reading surfaces over dashboard metrics, decorative charts, glass effects, or dense card grids.

### Design Principles

1. Start with the user’s next useful thought, not the size of the database.
2. Keep sources, reviewed knowledge, and AI candidates visibly distinct.
3. Every important relationship must explain why it exists and where its evidence comes from.
4. Use progressive disclosure: one framework first, nearby context second, the full graph only on demand.
5. Preserve spatial and interaction state so the workspace becomes familiar over time.
